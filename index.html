About

I am a middler at Northeastern University in Boston. Middler is a made-up word that means third year out of five. I am a Computer Engineering major with a Computer Science minor. I am a Sitecore Certified Professional Developer, and I have a strong interest in web technologies. When I’m not doing homework or at work, you can usually find me at a concert or helping plan a hackathon with my friends at HackBeanpot.

Work

As a Northeastern student, I have the opportunity to participate in three 6-month co-op positions. I recently finished my second co-op as a .NET Co-op at Genuine Interactive, a digital agency based in Boston, MA. In my time at Genuine, I participated in the development of three websites using platforms such as Sitecore and Umbraco.

For my first co-op I spent 6 months at a small startup named Viridian Spark. Viridian Spark was an implementer of Content Management Systems (CMS). At Viridian Spark, I was a member of the back-end development team. My duties included the creation of data templates and html renderings, bug fixing and support, daily scrums with the client, and end of sprint presentations of the work I had built. Viridian Spark was recently acquired by Verndale.

In previous summers (I think the count is up to 7) I worked as a Programming/Product Management Intern at Aras Corporation. Aras Corporation is a provider of enterprise Product Lifecycle Management software. My responsibilities at Aras varied each summer. I led development of two separate Silverlight applications. The first was a timesheet application to be used by internal contractors. The second was a Customer Relationship Management application designed for use by Aras’s partners. While at Aras, I also contributed to the development of a custom CMS platform built on Aras’s Innovator framework. Most recently, I was involved in the re-engineering of the Aras Community Forums. Originally based on a third-party system, the forums were redesigned to be driven by Innovator as well.
